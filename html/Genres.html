What Genre Are You?

Popping

A street dance style where dancers quickly contract and relax their muscles to create a jerking effect in their body, known as a "pop" or "hit." It's often done in sync with the beat of the music and includes other movements like waving, tutting, and robotic motions. The style focuses on sharp, rhythmic movements and illusion-like effects, making the dancer appear mechanical or animated.

Locking

A funky street dance style where dancers perform fast, exaggerated movements and then suddenly freeze in place, or “lock,” before continuing. It's known for its sharp pauses, distinct poses, and playful, expressive attitude. Locking includes moves like pointing, wrist rolls, and big arm swings, often performed to funk or old-school hip-hop music. The style emphasizes character, energy, and crowd interaction.

Hip-Hop Freestyle

A dynamic and expressive dance style that focuses on improvisation and personal style. Dancers move freely to the music, mixing various techniques like grooves, footwork, isolations, and body control. Unlike choreographed routines, freestyle is about feeling the beat, responding to the rhythm, and showcasing individuality. It draws from different street dance influences and lets dancers tell their own story through movement.
